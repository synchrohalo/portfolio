100 57 Day Project

Adobe Photoshop & Illustrator

Background

Around April last year, I attempted the 100 Day Project, my aim being to make an animated GIF everyday. Although I ended up with only 57 entries, doing this project rekindled my love for illustration and I've been trying to draw more often since.

About

I started the 100 Day Project to get myself out of a creative rut. For a long time before then, I hadn't been making anything for fun. This project was challenging—some days I felt inspired and, on others, had to force myself to sit down and complete an animation, whether or not I was happy with the result.

Like other artists, I constantly feel dissatisfaction with my creations. Over the course of the project, I gradually came to accept whatever I came up with and acknowledged that I could only get better from there. Here are some of my favorite GIFs from the project.

Day 10

Assorted Food

A collection of food animations I made on different days. Clockwise, from the top left, the images are of a siopao (the Filipino version of a baozi, or steamed bun), Korean tofu stew or soondubu, a cross section of a xiao long bao or soup dumpling, and Hong Kong egg waffles (called eggettes or egg puffs) taking shape on a pan.

Day 31

A rendering of my personal experience with social anxiety and issues with my sense of belonging.

Day 23

Day 46

A watermelon girl.
