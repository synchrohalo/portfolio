Davis Computer Science Club Banners

Adobe Photoshop & Illustrator

Background

The Davis Computer Science Club (DCSC) is an organization at UC Davis dedicated to serving CS students or those interested in the field. I was elected to be Marketing Director for the 2014-2015 school year, my responsibility mainly being to oversee the creation and distribution of marketing materials.

About

Although not formally a project, my role as Marketing Director with the Davis Computer Science Club was a year-long commitment that helped me grow as a designer and as a person.

Prior to my incumbency, my leadership skills were pretty much non-existent. Even at the beginning, I struggled to speak up during meetings because I was too shy and felt like I had nothing to contribute. I took the position primarily because of my interest in graphic design and concentrated mostly on that.

With a desire for change (and a little coaxing from the President at the time), I decided to step up and be more involved in my role. I proposed and planned a club t-shirt design contest and successfully ran a Super Smash Bros. Melee tournament in coordination with the quasi-Melee-club on campus. Together with two other officers, I organized a Gender Diversity in Technology event. I also took charge of generating club funds for the year.

Along with my personal growth, I tried to push myself to be a better designer as well. I began spending more and more time on each Facebook event banner, adding a focus on illustration. The following banners are my favorites among the bunch.

Winter Classes Preview

Every quarter, DCSC holds an event to discuss upcoming computer science courses and give students advice on what to expect. I decided to add the little drawing of the snowman on the side to make the banner look more fun and to make our club feel more welcoming.

January General Meeting

I made this during winter break, and, because I had more time on my hands, I went for a more illustration-heavy banner. I picked muted off-white colors for the background to make the text and the reds stand out. In hopes of increasing attendance, I added a cute character on the right with a speech bubble to give details about the event.

Spring Classes Preview

Another seasonal-themed piece. For the spring classes preview, I stuck with a more simple design, choosing to decorate the banner with geometrically "perfect" flowers and a little bee to break the uniformity.

Development Hub

Later during the school year, one of our officers had an idea for an event that would allow students of any major to get together once a week, form groups if desired, and just work on any projects of interest. This event was entitled Development Hub, or Dev Hub, for short. At the suggestion of a friend, I used a rocket ship to represent the idea of a launch pad, or, figuratively speaking, a good place to begin a new undertaking.

2015 Elections

My personal favorite. My color choices were meant to parallel those of the U.S. flag to evoke a patriotic and spirited response. The passing of the torch is a symbol commonly used to illustrate the handing down of roles, and I thought it appropriate for the image. I also wanted to suggest a sense of movement (with the positioning of the ribbon and the hands) to designate a "call to action" of sorts.

Joining DCSC was one of the best decisions I've ever made, and I'm glad to have been part of something so influential on a portion of the student population.
